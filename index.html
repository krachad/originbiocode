Meet the team →

Origin BioCode is a biological discovery platform operating in the Congo Basin. The company was founded on a premise that the world's most biologically complex region has been systematically overlooked by the life sciences industry — not because it lacks value, but because the infrastructure to access it responsibly did not exist.

The company operates under the Mwaana Inc. portfolio, with scientific operations led in partnership with UVU Bio and field operations anchored in Libreville and Cape Town. Discovery activities are conducted under a Memorandum of Understanding with AGADEV — Gabon's National Competent Authority under the Nagoya Protocol.

Origin BioCode is not a conservation initiative, an NGO, or a research project. It is a platform company building permanent scientific infrastructure in a frontier biological system.

The Region

The Congo Basin: the last great biological frontier.

The Congo Basin is the second-largest tropical rainforest on Earth, spanning six countries and covering over 3.7 million km². Despite its extraordinary biological complexity — harbouring an estimated share of the world's undescribed species — it remains dramatically underrepresented in the scientific literature.

Origin BioCode's field operations are currently focused on Gabon, one of the Congo Basin's most biodiverse and politically stable nations. Gabon has enacted world-leading legislation on access and benefit-sharing, making it the ideal entry point for responsible biological discovery.

All field activities are conducted under an MOU with AGADEV, ensuring full compliance with the Nagoya Protocol and Gabon's national legislation. Benefit-sharing structures are designed to create lasting value for local communities and scientific institutions.
